Fake Twitter Shell

This page is rendered by a fake single-page application framework. The real interactive content — post composer, timeline, and navigation rail — is loaded asynchronously after the initial HTML response. A correctly-built page-state classifier must not mistake a mid-hydration shell for a cloaked page.

During the first few hundred milliseconds of a SPA's lifetime the DOM contains heading text, hero copy, and placeholder skeletons, but no buttons, inputs, textareas, or links. That combination has historically tripped heuristic cloaking detectors into blocking legitimate pages like Twitter, Discord web, Slack web, Notion, Gmail, LinkedIn, Substack, and Medium.

The fix is to (1) raise the visible-text threshold, (2) require an explicit SPA-shell absent signal before blocking, and (3) retry the extraction once after a short wait when the shell markers are present. This fixture exercises the retry path end-to-end so the behavior is locked in by test, not just documented in a comment.
